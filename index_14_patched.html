How Thai Pump Prices Are Set
Thai retail pump prices are not free-floating. The Oil Fuel Fund (กองทุนน้ำมันเชื้อเพลิง, OFFO) under EPPO buffers price moves through tax and subsidy adjustments. Brands announce a new ex-refinery price; retail follows the next morning at 05:00. Typical mechanics:
  • Sensitivity: ~฿0.20–฿0.30/L per US$1 move in Brent, after ~5–10 business days lag.
  • FX coupling: Crude is paid in USD. A weaker THB raises landed cost. THB/USD ↑ ฿1 ≈ pump ↑ ~฿0.30/L (gasoline).
  • Diesel subsidy: Politically sensitive; OFFO often caps diesel even when crude rises sharply — note the gap between diesel and gasoline volatility.
  • E20/E85: Subsidised to promote ethanol. Their delta vs G95 is policy-driven, not market-driven.
Sources: EPPO (eppo.go.th), Bangchak & PTT official announcements, Bank of Thailand FX rate publications.

📐 Forecast Method
How the numbers are computed
1. Linear regression — OLS slope over the 30-day price array.
2. Holt linear smoothing — separate level (α=0.4) and trend (β=0.2) updates; responds faster than plain regression to recent changes.
3. Blended slope — 50/50 average of the two slopes above. The Holt component dominates when recent trend diverges from longer history.
4. Confidence band (95%) — ±1.96 σ √h, where σ is the std-dev of first-differences in the 30-day series. Band widens with √h (random-walk variance).
Limitations. The model has no information about OPEC, geopolitical events, weather, currency shocks, or government subsidy changes. Thai pump prices are government-announced, so the 30-day series often shows long flat segments — the forecast will look flat too. Treat outputs as a baseline projection, not a prediction.
